Background information about protein kinase inhibitors

A protein kinase inhibitor is a type of enzyme inhibitor that blocks the action of one or more protein kinases.

Protein kinase inhibitors are the front runners in signal transduction therapy, because the catalytic sites of enzymes are easier targets for drug design than proteinâ€“protein interaction domains.

The family of protein kinase inhibitors is a relatively new class of drugs, with new members being approved for use almost every year.

In addition, selective kinase inhibitors can often reveal unexpected biological insights, augmenting genetic approaches and playing a decisive role in preclinical target validation studies.

Nevertheless, developing protein kinase inhibitors with sufficient selectivity and pharmacodynamic potency presents significant challenges.
